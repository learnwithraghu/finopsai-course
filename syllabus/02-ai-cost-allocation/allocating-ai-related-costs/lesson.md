# Allocating AI-Related Costs

## Purpose
Establish consistent allocation models that translate GPU, data, and platform charges into accountable business units.

## Learning Objectives
- Identify direct vs. shared AI costs and decide on allocation drivers (GPU hours, tokens, experiments)
- Design tagging, chargeback, and showback patterns compatible with managed AI services and on-prem accelerators
- Enable finance partners to reconcile AI invoices with FinOps allocation data

## Discussion Topics
- Workload segmentation (training vs. inference vs. experimentation)
- Cost allocation techniques: proportional usage, reserved capacity amortization, fixed commitments
- Tooling considerations (FinOps platforms, custom billing pipelines)

## Practical Activities
- Document data sources feeding AI cost allocation and identify gaps
- Prototype a tagging taxonomy covering model, dataset, owner, and environment dimensions
- Simulate a cost allocation run and review variance with finance controllers

## Artifacts & Metrics
- AI tagging taxonomy
- Allocation decision log
- Finance variance analysis
